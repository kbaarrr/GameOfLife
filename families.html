Our most requested sessions

"She thought she would never see it again."

For many of our elders and patients, the deepest wish isn't a waterfall or a skyline — it's the Haram. Our sacred journeys bring the holy cities to the bedside in reverent, unhurried sessions: the call to prayer, the courtyards, the light. Families tell us these are the sessions where everyone in the room ends up in tears — the good kind.

Offered with humility, and clear limits A virtual journey is comfort, preparation and connection — it is not Hajj or Umrah, and it does not replace or fulfil any religious obligation. We present these sessions in consultation with religious scholars, and we will always say this plainly to every family.

How it works

We bring everything. You bring them.

1

Tell us about them

Who they are, where they are — home, hospital or care — and the places that matter to them. We'll plan the session around their energy and any medical guidance.

2

A facilitator comes to the bedside

With sanitised equipment, single-use covers, and time. Sessions run up to 90 minutes with as many pauses as needed. Family are part of it — a second screen lets everyone travel together.

3

The journey stays with them

We leave photographs of the moment and, for bespoke commissions, the film itself — the family's to keep, forever.
