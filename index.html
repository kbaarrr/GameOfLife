How a session works

A human brings the journey. Not a box.

Every Rihla session is run by a trained facilitator — because the magic isn't the headset, it's the hand on the shoulder.

1

We arrive prepared

Sanitised equipment, single-use hygiene covers, and protocols agreed with the ward or family beforehand.

2

The journey is chosen together

With the patient and family — a place they've missed, a dream they shelved, somewhere sacred.

3

A facilitator guides it

Narrating gently, watching comfort levels, pausing whenever needed. Sessions adapt to energy, mood and medical guidance.

4

The family shares it

A second screen mirrors the journey so everyone in the room travels together. The conversation after is half the medicine.

Care & conduct

Built for the most sensitive rooms in the world

🧼

Hygiene first

Hospital-grade sanitisation between every session, single-use face covers, and infection-control protocols agreed with clinical teams.

🫀

Clinical sensitivity

Facilitators are trained for fragile settings — motion-comfort screening, session length limits, and full deference to medical staff and family wishes.

🕌

Faith, with humility

Sacred journeys are offered as comfort and preparation — never as a substitute for pilgrimage — and we seek the counsel of religious scholars in how we present them.
